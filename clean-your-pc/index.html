Did you know?

Malicious Software (malware)

This software is a real invasion of nowadays: by using deceitful tactic it installs on your PC by itself and causes changes in your system (changes your settings, replaces software, causes errors, shows unethical ads, has access to your browsing habits and makes your computer easy-to-access for third persons).
It is much easier then you think to infect PC by malware by simply visiting an infected website, downloading infected software, or installing infected programs. Most antivirus programs can clean malware but it’s also a good idea to run an anti-malware or malware cleaner.

Potentially Unwanted Programs

PUP is an abbreviation for this unit, others use the term Potentially Unwanted Application (PUA) that is definitely the same: it is a program, that is not malicious by itself, but analysis of its behavior shows the symptoms of misconduct. Usually, it demonstrated as a kind of adware, miner, remote administration tool, bundled software, downloader, aggressive monetizing software, uninstall tool or any combination of them.

Browser Hijacks

Usually, users do not notice the moment when they install a program that actually replaces the real work of the browser and the most common symptoms are an advertisement in unexpected places and unethical content, inability to use familiar services, an abundance of malicious processes running on a computer and inability to stop all this. Often this happens after downloading cracked programs, using untrustworthy resources and installing unfair software. In fact, the resources of your computer (and often your personal data) are used by fraudsters for their personal purposes and to stop them as soon as possible is your first priority.
